How to do Sports Photography

By Matthew Means

Sports Photography 101

So you want to learn how to take sports pictures, or maybe not, but I guarantee if you follow these steps, and practice your craft, you can be a professional in no time!

  • 6 Tips for Success

  • Know the Sport
  • Have a Fast Camera
  • Motivation
  • Photoshop, Trust Me
  • Think Ahead

  1. Know the Sport, understand simple plays to follow along

    You can’t walk into a game without knowing how the sport is played. I’m not saying you need to be a former player or a former coach, it just makes it helpful, and your job easier if you know

  2. Have a fast camera, something that can capture the athletes running in motion

    You want to make sure you have a camera with a fast shutter speed to capture an image that won't show blur. Also, something with a high ISO because some arenas are darker than others and you need some lighting for your pictures

    >

  3. Motivation

    Focus on your work, not others. No matter what hobby you start, there will ALWAYS be someone better than you, and that's ok.

  4. Photoshop, Trust Me

    I know, photoshop sounds scary, but a little bit of editing goes a long way. You might have a really good camera and lens, but you will always get pictures that are too dark or too bright. Sometimes you want to make the white on a player's jersey stand out more or change the tinting to make the color of someone's skin look more natural in the human eye. You don't have to worry about making it look too fancy, just natural

  5. Think Ahead

    This is key. It's not easy, but it's possible to get good at. You need to know where to be at the right time in order to get the best shot, sometimes you need to get to a spot early, while most times you need to stay patient
